App Overview

Did your mouse suddenly break? Or maybe you just want to control your PC while laying in bed to watch movies? Phone Pad is the perfect utility. Instead of relying on buggy Bluetooth apps, Phone Pad uses your local Wi-Fi router to send touch inputs from your phone directly to your computer with virtually zero latency.

The app provides a spacious digital trackpad, left/right click buttons, and intuitive gesture support (like scrolling and zooming) right on your smartphone's display.

How to Use (Setup Guide)

  • Download the PC Server: Download the Phone Pad Server on your Windows PC using the button above.
  • Install & Open: Install and open the Server app on your PC. It will automatically initialize the connection bridge.
  • Check Network: Ensure both your Mobile phone and your PC are connected to the SAME Wi-Fi network (or connect your PC to your phone's hotspot).
  • Note the IP: Look at the PC Server app on your monitor and note down the local IP address displayed on the screen.
  • Connect: Open the Phone Pad app on your Android device, enter that exact IP address in the Home screen, and click Connect. You're ready to go!

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this require an active internet connection?
No! The connection is strictly local. As long as your phone and PC are connected to the same Wi-Fi router (even without internet data), the mouse will work perfectly.
Is the mouse movement laggy?
Unlike Bluetooth mice, Wi-Fi provides a much higher bandwidth. If you are on a decent router, the cursor movement will feel instantaneous and incredibly smooth.
Does it support scrolling and zooming?
Yes! The app supports standard multi-touch gestures. You can use two fingers to scroll up and down on web pages, or pinch to zoom.

Known Limitations

  • Same Network Required: The app will not work if your phone is on mobile data and your PC is on Wi-Fi. They must share the same local network.
  • Public Wi-Fi Blocks: Some public networks (like in cafes or colleges) enable "AP Isolation" which blocks devices from talking to each other. In this case, use your mobile hotspot instead.

Troubleshooting

Can't get the app to connect? Here are the most common solutions:

Connection Failed? (Firewall Issue) 90% of connection issues are caused by Windows Defender Firewall. When you open the PC Server for the first time, Windows will ask for permission. Make sure to click "Allow Access". If you missed it, go to Windows Firewall settings and allow the Phone Pad server through.
Cursor Lagging or Jumping? This means your Wi-Fi signal is weak. Try moving closer to your router, or switch your router connection from 2.4GHz to 5GHz for faster, uninterrupted input.
