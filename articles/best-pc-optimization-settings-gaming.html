How to Optimize PC Settings for Gaming: The Ultimate Performance Guide

Published by BSR Studios • March 20, 2026 👁️ Loading...

You have updated your drivers, closed your browser tabs, and lowered your resolution, but your games are still stuttering. Worse yet, you are experiencing "input lag"—that split-second delay between clicking your mouse and seeing your gun fire on screen.

To achieve peak performance, basic tweaks are not enough. You need to dive deep into Windows settings to stop the OS from bottlenecking your hardware. Whether you have a robust RTX setup or a budget laptop with integrated graphics, applying these specific PC optimization settings will help you unlock better FPS and smoother gameplay.

1 Enable Hardware-Accelerated GPU Scheduling (HAGS)

This is one of the most powerful hidden features in Windows 10 and 11. Enabling HAGS allows your graphics card to manage its own memory independently of Windows, effectively reducing latency and boosting performance in heavy titles.

  • Press the Windows key, type "Graphics settings", and press Enter.
  • At the top, look for Hardware-accelerated GPU scheduling.
  • Toggle the switch to On.
  • Restart your PC to apply the changes. (Note: This requires a relatively modern GPU and updated drivers).

2 Disable Xbox Game Bar & Background Captures

Windows has a built-in feature that constantly records your screen in the background just in case you want to save a quick clip. This feature silently utilizes your CPU and disk, which can cause stuttering in competitive games.

  • Press Windows Key + I to open Settings.
  • Go to the Gaming section.
  • Click on Xbox Game Bar and toggle it to Off.
  • Next, click on Captures (on the left menu) and ensure "Record in the background while I'm playing a game" is set to Off.

3 Optimize NVIDIA/AMD Control Panel Settings

Your GPU has its own control panel that overrides in-game settings. For better performance, you can configure it to prioritize speed over visual quality.

  • For NVIDIA Users: Right-click your desktop and open NVIDIA Control Panel. Go to "Manage 3D settings." Set Power management mode to Prefer maximum performance. Set Texture filtering - Quality to High performance.
  • For AMD Users: Open the AMD Radeon Software. Go to the Gaming tab. Disable Radeon Chill and Radeon Anti-Lag (they sometimes cause stuttering on older PCs). Set Texture Filtering Quality to Performance.

4 Turn Off Mouse Acceleration (Enhance Aim)

If you play FPS games like Valorant, CS:GO, or Apex Legends, "Mouse Acceleration" can be problematic. It inconsistently changes your crosshair speed based on how fast you move your hand, affecting your muscle memory.

  • Press the Windows Key, type "Mouse settings", and press Enter.
  • Click on Additional mouse options on the right side.
  • Go to the Pointer Options tab.
  • Uncheck the box that says "Enhance pointer precision."
  • Click Apply and OK. Now you have a more consistent raw input.

5 Replace Heavy Apps with Efficient Alternatives

You can optimize Windows extensively, but if you have heavy applications like Discord, Spotify, or heavy recording software running in the background, your game might still struggle.

  • Live Wallpapers: Don't use heavy web-based wallpaper engines that utilize excess RAM. Switch to AnyVidWall or AnyWebWall. They feature a Smart Gaming Mode that auto-pauses the wallpaper the moment you launch a game, ensuring a minimal CPU footprint.
  • Recording Gameplay: Consider lightweight alternatives for recording on budget PCs. Use Any Screen Recorder instead. It records uncompressed raw data directly, resulting in highly efficient CPU usage while recording gameplay.

By applying these optimization settings, you can help reduce input lag, stabilize your frame times, and ensure your PC runs at its best possible performance.
