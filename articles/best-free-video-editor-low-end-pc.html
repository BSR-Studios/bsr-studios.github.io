How to Edit Videos on a Low-End PC: Top 5 Free Editors (2026)

By BSR Studios • March 20, 2026 👁️ Loading...

You have recorded a fantastic gameplay session or shot a great vlog, but when you try to open heavy video editing software on your laptop, your PC freezes completely. High-end video editors demand a massive amount of RAM and a dedicated graphics card just to play the video timeline smoothly.

If you are working with a budget system—a computer with only 2GB or 4GB of RAM and integrated graphics—you need software that is highly optimized. You don't have to give up on creating content! Here is a guide on how to choose and use the top 5 free video editors that perform efficiently on entry-level machines.

1 CapCut (PC Version)

Originally a mobile app, the CapCut PC version is heavily optimized, making it quite responsive even on older laptops. It features a drag-and-drop interface, extensive libraries of free transitions, trending effects, and built-in Auto-Captions, which are perfect for YouTube Shorts and TikToks.

Best For: Fast-paced edits, YouTube Shorts, and social media content.
RAM Needed: Performs well on 4GB RAM setups.

2 VSDC Free Video Editor

If you are looking for how to edit on an entry-level system (like a laptop with an older processor), VSDC is a highly recommended choice. It is a non-linear editor that is deeply optimized for legacy hardware. It handles HD files efficiently and exports your final video without adding a watermark.

Best For: Entry-level PCs, basic cutting, and color correction.
RAM Needed: Functions reliably on 2GB RAM.

3 Shotcut

Shotcut is a robust open-source video editor that doesn't strictly require a dedicated graphics card to function. While the interface might feel traditional, it is packed with professional features like audio mixing, keyframing, and hundreds of filters. It is completely free and community-driven.

Best For: Users seeking advanced features without heavy system requirements.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM is recommended for optimal performance.

4 OpenShot

If you have never edited a video before and want to know how to start quickly, OpenShot is a great entry point. It is an open-source tool designed to be easy to learn. You can slice video, add text titles, and export efficiently. Its minimal background footprint makes it suitable for older hardware.

Best For: Complete beginners, simple trimming, and fast exporting.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM recommended.

5 Kdenlive

Kdenlive is a powerful open-source editor offering a multi-track timeline. The key feature for low-end PC users is its built-in "Proxy Editing". If you want to know how to edit 1080p footage without stuttering, Proxy Editing creates temporary, low-resolution copies of your files so you can edit smoothly, and then renders the final video in full quality.

Best For: Advanced editing workflows and utilizing Proxy files.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM minimum.
