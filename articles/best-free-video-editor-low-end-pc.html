Top 5 Free Video Editors for Low End PC (2GB/4GB RAM)

Published by BSR Studios • March 20, 2026

You have recorded a fantastic gameplay session or shot a great vlog, but when you try to open Adobe Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve on your laptop, your PC freezes completely. High-end video editors demand a massive amount of RAM and a dedicated graphics card just to play the video timeline smoothly.

If you are working with a "Potato PC"—a system with only 2GB or 4GB of RAM and Intel HD Graphics—you need software that is incredibly lightweight. You don't have to give up on your YouTube dreams! Here are the top 5 best, 100% free video editors that run like butter on low-end machines.

1 CapCut (PC Version)

Originally a mobile app, the CapCut PC version has taken the creator world by storm. It is heavily optimized, making it extremely snappy even on older laptops. It features a drag-and-drop interface, massive libraries of free transitions, trending effects, and built-in Auto-Captions for YouTube Shorts and TikToks.

Best For: Fast-paced edits, YouTube Shorts, TikToks, and Anime montages.
RAM Needed: Runs decently on 4GB RAM.

2 VSDC Free Video Editor

If you have an absolute potato PC (we are talking 2GB RAM and an old 3rd-Gen Intel processor), VSDC is your best friend. It is a non-linear editor that has been around for years, meaning it is deeply optimized for older hardware. It handles 4K and HD files smoothly and exports without a watermark.

Best For: Extremely low-end PCs, basic cutting, and color correction.
RAM Needed: Runs smoothly on just 2GB RAM.

3 Shotcut

Shotcut is a fantastic open-source video editor. It doesn't require a high-end graphics card to function. While the interface might feel a little "old school" compared to CapCut, it is packed with professional features like audio mixing, keyframing, and hundreds of filters. It is completely free with zero ads or watermarks.

Best For: Users who want a Premiere Pro feel without the heavy system requirements.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM is recommended.

4 OpenShot

If you have never edited a video in your life and want something incredibly simple, OpenShot is the answer. It is an open-source tool designed to be easy to learn and quick to use. You can slice video, add text titles, and export quickly. It is very lightweight and runs perfectly on old hardware.

Best For: Complete beginners, simple trimming, and fast rendering.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM recommended.

5 Kdenlive

Kdenlive is a powerful open-source editor that boasts a professional multi-track timeline. The secret weapon of Kdenlive for low-end PCs is its built-in "Proxy Editing" feature. It automatically creates tiny, low-resolution copies of your video files so you can edit smoothly without lag, and then renders the final video in full high quality.

Best For: Advanced open-source editing and Proxy workflow.
RAM Needed: 4GB RAM minimum.
