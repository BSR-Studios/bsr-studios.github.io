Top 10 Free Open World Games for Low End PC (2GB/4GB RAM)

Published by BSR Studios • March 20, 2026

Every gamer dreams of exploring massive, endless open worlds like GTA 5, Skyrim, or Cyberpunk 2077. But when you are gaming on a "Potato PC"—an old laptop with just 2GB or 4GB of RAM and no dedicated graphics card (Intel HD)—launching these AAA games will instantly turn your PC into a heater.

Don't worry! You don't need a $1000 gaming rig to enjoy an expansive open-world experience. The gaming community and indie developers have created some incredibly massive, 100% free games that run smoothly on practically any machine. Here is the ultimate list of the top 10 best free open-world games for low-end PCs.

1 Unturned

Don't let the blocky graphics fool you. Unturned is one of the deepest, most feature-rich multiplayer open-world zombie survival games on Steam. You can drive cars, build massive bases, craft weapons, and fight both zombies and other players across giant maps.

Genre: Survival Zombie RPG
Min RAM: 2 GB
Graphics: Runs smoothly on ancient Intel HD Graphics.

2 Old School RuneScape (OSRS)

OSRS is legendary. It is a massive multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) with a world that takes hours to walk across. From cutting trees and fishing to fighting epic bosses and trading with thousands of real players, this game is a masterpiece that can run on literally any toaster.

Genre: Classic MMORPG
Min RAM: 512 MB
Graphics: None required. If your PC can turn on, it can run OSRS.

3 Trove

If you love the voxel-style building of Minecraft but want the combat, dungeons, and loot of an RPG, Trove is the perfect game. You can pick different classes (like a Ninja or Pirate), explore massive randomly generated worlds, ride dragons, and build your own "Cornerstone" house.

Genre: Voxel Action MMO
Min RAM: 2 GB
Graphics: Intel HD 4000 or better.

4 Warframe

Wait, Warframe on a low-end PC? Yes! The developers of Warframe possess actual magic when it comes to optimization. While it is a fast-paced "Space Ninja" game with stunning graphics, if you turn the settings down to low, it runs incredibly well on 4GB RAM setups. The open-world areas (like Plains of Eidolon) are massive and 100% free.

Genre: Action Sci-Fi Looter Shooter
Min RAM: 4 GB
Graphics: DirectX 11 compatible integrated graphics.

5 Albion Online

Albion Online is a hardcore fantasy sandbox MMORPG where the economy is driven entirely by players. Every piece of armor and weapon is crafted by a player. You can buy a private island, farm crops, or risk everything in massive open-world PvP battles. The top-down isometric graphics make it super easy to run.

Genre: Isometric Sandbox MMORPG
Min RAM: 4 GB
Graphics: Intel HD Graphics 3000 or newer.

6 Minetest

Can't afford Minecraft? Minetest is a completely free, open-source voxel game engine. Out of the box, it is a massive infinite open world. The best part is the massive community: you can download hundreds of free mods directly from the main menu to turn it into a survival game, a puzzle game, or a city builder. It uses a fraction of the RAM that Java Minecraft uses.

Genre: Open Source Voxel Sandbox
Min RAM: 1 GB
Graphics: Runs on any ancient integrated graphics.

7 The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall

Before Skyrim, there was Daggerfall. Bethesda has released this legendary 1996 classic completely for free on Steam. It features a procedurally generated open world that is literally the size of Great Britain! You can explore thousands of towns, dungeons, and secrets. Since it is a retro game, it requires basically zero system resources.

Genre: Classic Retro RPG
Min RAM: 512 MB
Graphics: No GPU required.

8 Roblox (Open World Servers)

Roblox isn't just a game; it is an engine. If you want open worlds, search for games inside Roblox like "Jailbreak," "Blox Fruits," or "Brookhaven." Millions of players play these massive open-world simulators every day. The engine is heavily optimized to run on low-end laptops and mobile phones alike.

Genre: Multiplayer Sandbox Engine
Min RAM: 2 GB
Graphics: DirectX 10 minimum (Intel HD).

9 Guild Wars 2 (Base Game)

The entire base game of Guild Wars 2 is now free to play. It offers one of the most beautiful fantasy open worlds ever created, featuring dynamic events where hundreds of players group up to fight massive dragons. By lowering the graphics and character model limits in the settings, it runs surprisingly well on 4GB setups.

Genre: Fantasy MMORPG
Min RAM: 4 GB
Graphics: Intel HD 3000 or better.

10 Muck

Created by famous YouTuber "Dani" as a joke, Muck turned into one of the most fun, chaotic, and challenging free open-world survival roguelikes on Steam. You gather resources during the day, build a base, and fight off hordes of terrifying monsters at night. The simplistic graphics mean your potato PC will handle it perfectly.

Genre: Survival Roguelike
Min RAM: 2 GB
Graphics: Dual Core integrated graphics.
