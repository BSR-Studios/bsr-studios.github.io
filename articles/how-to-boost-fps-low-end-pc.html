How to Boost FPS on a Low-End PC (Performance Guide)

Published by BSR Studios • March 20, 2026 👁️ Loading...

Playing games on a budget laptop or a PC with only 2GB to 4GB of RAM can be incredibly frustrating. Stuttering screens, massive frame drops during intensive moments, and generally low framerates can significantly impact your gaming experience.

But before assuming you need to buy an expensive gaming rig, consider optimizing your current setup. Windows OS is full of hidden bottlenecks. By applying a few smart, system-level tweaks, you can help your hardware deliver better performance. This guide will show you exactly how to optimize your PC for gaming and achieve smoother gameplay.

1 How to Enable High Performance Power Plan

By default, Windows sets your PC's power plan to "Balanced" to save electricity and battery life. This setting can restrict your CPU and GPU from reaching their maximum clock speeds during gaming sessions.

  • Press the Windows key, type "Choose a power plan" and press Enter.
  • Click on the dropdown arrow that says "Show additional plans."
  • Select the High Performance option. (If you are on a laptop, ensure your charger is plugged in while gaming to prevent rapid battery drain).

2 How to Update Your Graphics Drivers

Running games on outdated graphics drivers (like older Intel HD Graphics drivers) is a common cause of low FPS and visual glitches. GPU manufacturers regularly release updates specifically designed to optimize performance for new games.

  • Right-click the Windows Start button and open Device Manager.
  • Expand the Display Adapters section.
  • Right-click your graphics card (Intel, AMD, or NVIDIA) and select Update driver.
  • Choose "Search automatically for drivers." (For optimal results, consider downloading the official updater software like NVIDIA GeForce Experience or Intel Driver Support Assistant).

3 How to Turn On Windows Game Mode

Microsoft integrated a dedicated "Game Mode" into Windows 10 and 11. When activated, it instructs Windows to prioritize your game's processing needs and suspends resource-heavy background activities like Windows Update downloads.

  • Press Windows Key + I to open Settings.
  • Click on the Gaming category.
  • On the left menu, select Game Mode.
  • Toggle the switch to On.

4 How to Adjust In-Game Resolution & Graphics

Playing at 1080p on a system with limited RAM is often too demanding. Resolution determines how many pixels your GPU must render every second, making it the most impactful setting to change.

  • Open your game's Video/Graphics settings menu.
  • Lower your resolution from 1080p (1920x1080) to 720p (1280x720). This adjustment can significantly improve your framerate.
  • Turn off heavy post-processing effects like Anti-Aliasing (AA), Shadows, and Motion Blur. Set overall texture quality to Low or Medium.
