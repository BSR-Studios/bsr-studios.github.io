Customizing a Windows PC goes beyond changing taskbar colors or adjusting themes. Today, one of the most popular ways to enhance your digital workspace is by learning how to set a video wallpaper on PC. Whether you want a looping cyberpunk cityscape, a relaxing anime rain scene, or a custom MP4 file playing on your desktop, motion backgrounds completely change the feel of your computer.

However, if you are using a budget laptop or an older desktop PC, you might have noticed that playing a video as your wallpaper causes your system to slow down. In this guide, we will explain how background video rendering works and show you how to set up motion wallpapers efficiently without causing game lag.

1. The Rise of Video Wallpapers

Static images are great, but video wallpapers add a layer of immersion. From cinematic edits to seamless motion graphics, having an MP4 playing silently in the background creates an engaging aesthetic.

But how do you actually put a video on your desktop? Windows natively only supports static images (JPEG, PNG). To display video, you need a third-party application known as a wallpaper engine that constantly paints the video frames onto the lowest layer of your screen.

2. How Standard Wallpaper Engines Impact Performance

If you have ever wondered how to fix game stuttering while running a live wallpaper, you need to understand how mainstream engines operate.

Many popular applications use hidden web browsers (Chromium-based frameworks) to decode and play videos. This means that to display a simple MP4 file, your computer is essentially running an invisible web browser 24/7. This consumes a significant amount of RAM and requires your CPU to constantly decode the video stream.

"Running a heavy browser process just to loop a video background is incredibly inefficient. When you open a heavy game, your processor is forced to split its power between the game and the wallpaper."
