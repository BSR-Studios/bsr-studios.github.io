How to Record Gameplay Smoothly: OBS vs Any Screen Recorder

Published by BSR Studios • March 20, 2026 👁️ Loading...

You have an excellent animated background you want to share, or you finally achieved a stable 60 FPS in your favorite competitive game on an older laptop. Naturally, you want to learn how to record your screen to share these moments. Many turn to the industry-standard software: OBS Studio (Open Broadcaster Software).

However, users often encounter a major issue. You hit record, and instantly, your system struggles. The mouse response becomes sluggish, audio may stutter, and your in-game framerate drops significantly. The recorded video often appears choppy, failing to reflect your actual gameplay.

If this sounds familiar, you are facing common budget PC challenges. Professional screen recording software is generally built assuming the user has access to modern multi-core processors and abundant RAM. They are not always optimized for systems with only 2GB or 4GB of RAM. In this guide, we will compare OBS Studio against Any Screen Recorder to help you learn how to choose the most efficient recording method for your hardware.

1. How OBS Studio Operates on High-End PCs

There is no denying that OBS Studio is a powerhouse in the recording and streaming landscape. It is open-source, features extensive mixing tools, scene management, and professional plugin support. If you are learning how to set up a professional stream on a high-end gaming rig (16GB+ RAM, dedicated GPU), OBS is highly recommended.

Why OBS Causes Stuttering on Budget Systems

That immense capability requires significant system resources. When you launch OBS on a Windows 10 machine with limited RAM, the background processes alone can consume 400MB to 600MB of your available memory. Furthermore, mainstream encoding frameworks used within OBS are extremely CPU-intensive, as they decode and compress screen data into an MP4 video in real-time.

On a budget system, this lack of available memory forces Windows to utilize "Page Files" (using the much slower hard drive as emergency RAM). This massive CPU bottleneck and constant disk usage are the primary causes of severe in-game lag spikes during recording.

2. How Any Screen Recorder Optimizes Capture

We built Any Screen Recorder with a single objective: to learn how to ensure any PC, regardless of its hardware limitations, can capture smooth gameplay or desktop footage. We shifted the focus away from live broadcasting and scene mixing, concentrating purely on recording efficiency for 2GB and 4GB RAM systems.

Any Screen Recorder features a minimalistic UI designed to minimize system resource usage.

How the "Raw Capture" Method Works

Unlike traditional tools, Any Screen Recorder separates the screen data capture phase from the file compression phase. Our application is built on native C++ with Direct3D APIs that communicate directly with your graphics card.

During a session, Any Screen Recorder creates a proprietary raw .screc file. Crucially, it does not compress this data while you are actively playing. By deferring the compression, we achieve an exceptionally low CPU usage—often just 2% to 3%. Once you finish your task, the software allows you to "Turn into Video," safely compressing the raw data into an MP4 without disrupting your active gameplay.

4. How to Manage Raw File Sizes

When learning how to record efficiently with this method, there is one important consideration to keep in mind. Because Any Screen Recorder saves raw, uncompressed .screc data initially to ensure smooth performance, the intermediate temporary files are quite large.

One minute of raw capture can take around 80MB of storage space, requiring adequate write speeds on your storage drive. However, once you use the built-in converter, the final MP4 will be compressed down to a standard, easily shareable size.

5. How to Choose What's Right For You

The choice depends on your specific needs and hardware capabilities. If you are a streamer needing complex scene transitions and overlays, OBS Studio remains an excellent tool, provided you have the hardware to support it.

However, if you are working with a budget laptop or a 2GB/4GB RAM system and simply want to learn how to record tutorials or gameplay smoothly without impacting your PC's speed, Any Screen Recorder is a highly recommended choice. The deferred compression technology provides a level of efficiency that traditional encoders cannot replicate on low-end hardware.
